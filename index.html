Nicholas Ham

Personal site of Nicholas Ham. I hold a doctorate in mathematics from the University of Tasmania, during which I had Desmond FitzGerald and Peter Jarvis (Google Scholar) as advisors.

Amongst other open source projects, I develop a non-opinionated high-performance web development framework Nift.

On top of completing a mathematics major during undergrad, I completed majors in analytical economics and applied computing. I also contributed to designing and building an international programming competition sponsored by Google, and have experience teaching both mathematics and economics classes at the undergraduate level.

I enjoy playing around with generative art (examples) using an existing image or video as a canvas along with opencv to access the RGB values at each pixel, coding c/c++ to generate frames using various of my own algorithmic techniques along with custom RGB↔HSV conversion algorithms (none of these work correctly), then combine the generated frames using ffmpeg, similar to this. See also algorithmic art, computer art, digital art, electronic art and artificial intelligence art.

I am also currently working with a machine learning/artificial intelligence startup wearing multiple hats - developer, frontend/backend/cloud engineer & devops/mlops among other responsibilities.
